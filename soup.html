Tomato Soup

Tomato soup is a beloved classic that embodies comforting flavors and a heartwarming essence. It begins with a sauté of onions, garlic, carrots, and celery, creating a fragrant base. Canned tomatoes, either whole or diced, are then introduced, their acidity balanced with a touch of sugar. A medley of dried herbs, including basil and oregano, infuses the soup with savory undertones. After a gentle simmer, the soup is transformed into velvety perfection through blending, resulting in a smooth and luscious consistency. For those seeking extra indulgence, a swirl of heavy cream can be added, lending a creamy richness to the mix. Whether served as a stand-alone comfort food or as a delightful companion to grilled cheese, tomato soup never fails to provide a warm and soothing culinary experience. Garnished with fresh basil or parsley, it embodies the timeless essence of homemade comfort in a bowl.

prep time

45 min.

cook time

35 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : food Breakfast

instructions

step 1

Sauté the Vegetables: Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, diced carrot, and diced celery. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are softened and the onion is translucent.

step 2

Add Tomatoes: Add the canned tomatoes (including their juice) to the pot. If you're using whole tomatoes, break them up with a spoon or spatula. Stir in the sugar (if using) to balance the acidity of the tomatoes.

step 3

Season and Simmer: Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth and season with salt, black pepper, dried basil, and dried oregano. Stir well. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

step 4

Blend the Soup: After simmering, use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, carefully transfer the soup in batches to a countertop blender and blend until smooth. If using a countertop blender, be cautious as hot liquids can create pressure. You can leave the soup slightly chunky if you prefer a textured consistency.

step 4

Finish with Cream (Optional): Return the blended soup to the pot. If you want a creamy tomato soup, stir in the heavy cream and heat the soup over low heat until it's warmed through. Adjust the seasoning if necessary.

ingredients

2-3 green chilies, slit

1 medium-sized onion, thinly sliced

1 medium-sized capsicum (green bell pepper), thinly sliced

1 tablespoon tomato ketchup

1 tablespoon red chili sauce

1 onion, finely chopped

2 tablespoons olive oil

2 cloves garlic, minced

1 teaspoon sugar

Salt to taste

tools

Hand Blender
