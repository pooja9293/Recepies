Pancakes

Pancakes, cherished as a breakfast classic around the world, are a delightful treat loved for their simplicity and versatility. This beloved dish starts with a basic batter, a harmonious blend of all-purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and a pinch of salt. The magic unfolds when this dry mixture is gently combined with a wet mixture featuring buttermilk (or a homemade buttermilk substitute), an egg, melted butter, and optional vanilla extract for a touch of sweetness.As the batter sizzles on a hot griddle or skillet, it undergoes a captivating transformation. Bubbles rise to the surface, signaling the perfect moment for a flip, resulting in pancakes that are golden brown on both sides. The outcome is a stack of fluffy, tender rounds that can be customized to suit individual tastes, whether adorned with a drizzle of maple syrup, a sprinkle of chocolate chips, or a cascade of fresh fruits.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

5 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : food Breakfast

instructions

step 1

Preheat the Griddle or Pan: Preheat a non-stick griddle or a large skillet over medium-high heat. You can test if it's hot enough by sprinkling a few drops of water onto the surface; if they sizzle and evaporate, it's ready.

step 2

Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

step 3

Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk the buttermilk (or milk and vinegar/lemon juice mixture), egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract (if using).

step 4

Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Stir gently until just combined. It's okay if there are a few lumps in the batter; overmixing can make the pancakes less fluffy.

step 4

Grease the Griddle or Pan: Lightly grease the hot griddle or pan with a small amount of cooking oil or melted butter.

ingredients

1 cup all-purpose flour

2 tablespoons granulated sugar

1 teaspoon baking powder

1/2 teaspoon baking soda

1/4 teaspoon salt

1 cup buttermilk (or 1 cup milk with 1 tablespoon white vinegar or lemon juice, stirred and left to sit for 5 minutes)

1 large egg

2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted

1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Cooking oil or additional melted butter for the pan

tools

Hand Blender

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups

Large pot
